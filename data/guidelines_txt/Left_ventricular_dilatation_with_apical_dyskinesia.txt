**Typical features**

* Echo often shows **enlarged LV cavity size** with **apical segment(s) bulging outward in systole** (dyskinesia).
* Global systolic function may be **reduced**, but can vary; apical dyskinesia often indicates **regional scar/remodeling**.
* May be associated with **secondary (functional) mitral regurgitation** due to LV dilation and leaflet tethering, though this is not always present.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Ischemic cardiomyopathy** with apical scar/aneurysmal remodeling
* **Dilated cardiomyopathy** with regional variation
* **Prior myocarditis** with residual regional dysfunction
* **Takotsubo cardiomyopathy** (if acute/subacute context)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* “Dilatation” should be interpreted relative to **body size** and imaging quality; measurements can vary by method.
* Apical dyskinesia can be misread if the apex is **foreshortened**.

**Typical features**

* Echo suggests **intact chordae tendineae** and papillary–chordal mechanics, without signs of:

  * **Chordal rupture**
  * **Flail leaflet motion**
  * **Abnormal chordal thickening or restricted motion** in the assessed region
* Mitral subvalvular apparatus appears **functionally preserved**, even if nearby LV segments are abnormal.

**Common differential diagnoses (when dysfunction is suspected but not seen)**

* **Mitral regurgitation due to annular dilation/tethering** (functional MR rather than chordal problem)
* **Leaflet pathology** (degenerative or rheumatic) with preserved chordal motion
* **Imaging artifact** limiting chordal visualization

**Notes / uncertainty**

* Chordae can be hard to visualize; “normal function” may reflect **limited acoustic windows** rather than definitive exclusion of subtle pathology.

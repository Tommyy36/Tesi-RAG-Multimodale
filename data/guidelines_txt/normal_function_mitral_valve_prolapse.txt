**Typical features**

* Echo may show **systolic billowing/prolapse** of one or both mitral leaflets into the left atrium.
* “Normal function” often implies **no hemodynamically significant mitral regurgitation** on Doppler (or only minimal jets).
* LV size and systolic function are often **preserved**, though this can vary by patient.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Myxomatous degenerative mitral disease** (spectrum from mild to severe MR)
* **Functional (secondary) MR** (leaflets may appear abnormal in tethering but not true prolapse)
* **Normal variant billowing** (especially if criteria are borderline)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* MR severity can be **dynamic** and load-dependent; a “normal function” label may not exclude progression over time.

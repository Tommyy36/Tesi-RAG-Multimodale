**Typical features**

* Echo shows **marked septal thickening** with **preserved global systolic function** by conventional measures.
* Important accompanying assessments often include:

  * Presence/absence of **dynamic LV outflow tract (LVOT) obstruction**
  * **Systolic anterior motion (SAM)** of the mitral valve
  * **Diastolic dysfunction** and elevated filling pressure indicators
* Regional function may appear normal, while symptoms can still occur due to obstruction/diastolic issues.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy** (classic consideration with severe septal hypertrophy)
* **Long-standing hypertension** with asymmetric remodeling
* **Aortic stenosis**–related hypertrophy
* **Storage/infiltrative diseases** (selected contexts)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* “Normal function” (normal EF) can be misleading in severe hypertrophy; clinical impact may relate more to **obstruction and diastolic function** than EF.
* Definitive characterization often benefits from **CMR** (fibrosis/scar patterns) and clinical correlation.

**Typical features**

* Echo often shows **septal (and often concentric) hypertrophy** as an adaptive response to pressure overload.
* LV systolic function can remain **preserved** for a long time; diastolic dysfunction may be present even with normal EF.
* Evaluation commonly focuses on **aortic valve morphology and hemodynamic severity** (without assuming specific thresholds here).

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Hypertensive heart disease**
* **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy** (especially if hypertrophy is disproportionate)
* **Athlete’s heart** (if clinical context fits)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* Apparent “normal function” may mask **subclinical systolic impairment** detectable by strain imaging.
* Severity of hypertrophy and function depend on duration and severity of afterload and comorbidities.

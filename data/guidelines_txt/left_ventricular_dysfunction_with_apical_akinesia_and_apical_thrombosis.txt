**Typical features**

* Echo shows **reduced LV systolic function** plus **apical akinesia** (near-absent systolic thickening/motion).
* A **discrete echogenic apical mass** may be seen, often layered against the endocardium, consistent with **apical thrombus**.
* **Low apical flow / spontaneous echo contrast** may be present, supporting blood stasis.
* Contrast echo may improve visualization when the apex is poorly defined.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Ischemic cardiomyopathy / prior anterior MI** (classic association)
* **Takotsubo cardiomyopathy** (can be complicated by thrombus in some cases)
* **Apical aneurysm** (thrombus within aneurysmal segment)
* **LV noncompaction / prominent trabeculation** (may mimic thrombus)
* **Apical tumor or vegetation** (less common; considered when appearance/attachment is atypical)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* Standard echo can **miss or misclassify** apical thrombus; **contrast echo or cardiac MRI** is often used when doubt remains.
* Thrombus likelihood rises with **apical akinesia and low-flow states**, but exact risk varies.

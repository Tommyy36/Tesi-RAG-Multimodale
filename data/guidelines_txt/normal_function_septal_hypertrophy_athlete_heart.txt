**Typical features**

* Echo may show **increased septal thickness** consistent with **physiologic remodeling**, often with:

  * **Normal or hyperdynamic LV systolic function**
  * **Normal chamber compliance markers** (often, but not always)
  * Possible **balanced remodeling** (cavity size and wall thickness adapted to training type)
* No typical features of obstruction are expected, though this depends on anatomy.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)** (key alternative, especially if hypertrophy is marked/asymmetric)
* **Hypertensive heart disease**
* **Aortic stenosis–related hypertrophy**
* **Infiltrative cardiomyopathy** (context-dependent)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* Differentiating athlete’s heart from HCM may require **de-training response**, family history, ECG, strain, or **CMR**.
* “Normal function” does not exclude subtle abnormalities (e.g., diastolic impairment in some athletes).

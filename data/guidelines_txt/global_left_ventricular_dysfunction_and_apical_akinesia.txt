**Typical features**

* Echo shows **diffuse LV systolic impairment** plus a **more severe regional deficit at the apex** (akinesia).
* The apex may show **reduced thickening** and contribute disproportionately to reduced overall performance.
* May predispose to **apical stasis** and possible thrombus, depending on severity.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Ischemic cardiomyopathy** with predominant apical infarction plus more generalized dysfunction
* **Dilated cardiomyopathy** with regional scar/previous injury
* **Takotsubo cardiomyopathy** (classically apical involvement; global impairment can be present)
* **Myocarditis** with mixed global/regional pattern

**Notes / uncertainty**

* Mixed global + regional patterns often require correlation with **coronary territory**, timing, ECG, and biomarkers.
* Apical akinesia can be **under- or overestimated** if apical views are suboptimal.

**Typical features**

* Echo typically shows **diffuse reduction of LV systolic performance** (global hypokinesia), often with reduced contractility across multiple segments.
* LV cavity may be **normal-sized or dilated**, depending on acuity and chronicity.
* May have supportive findings such as **left atrial enlargement** or **diastolic dysfunction patterns**, though these are variable.

**Common differential diagnoses**

* **Dilated cardiomyopathy** (idiopathic/genetic/toxic)
* **Ischemic cardiomyopathy** (multivessel or diffuse scar)
* **Myocarditis / inflammatory cardiomyopathy**
* **Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy**
* **Infiltrative or metabolic cardiomyopathies** (context-dependent)

**Notes / uncertainty**

* “Global dysfunction” is a **functional description**, not a diagnosis; etiology often requires clinical context and additional testing.
* Some conditions show **load-dependence** (e.g., blood pressure, volume status) that can change apparent function.
